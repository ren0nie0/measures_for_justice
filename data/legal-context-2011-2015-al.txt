Legal Context - Alabama

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [Ala. Code § 11-45-9.1(a) (2015)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Class C misdemeanors. [Ala. Code § 11-45-9.1(a) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Offenses involving violence, threat of violence, or alcohol or drugs.
        [Ala. Code § 11-45-9.1(a) (2015)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [Ala. Code § 11-45-9.1(a) (2015)]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - Any law enforcement officer. [Ala. Code § 11-45-9.1(a) (2015)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [Ala. Code § 13A-12-214 (2015)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ala. Code §
        15-27-1(a) (2015),Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - The case was dismissed with prejudice. [Ala. Code § 15-27-1(a)
        (2015),Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant is found not guilty. [Ala. Code § 15-27-1(a) (2015),Ala.
        Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - One year has elapsed since the successful completion of a diversion
        program. [Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a)(3)(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Ala. Code § 15-27-4(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner does not owe any restitution, fines, court costs, late
        fees, or other fees. [Ala. Code § 15-27-12 (2015)]
      - The offense for which the person was arrested was not a violent offense.
        [Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Ala. Code § 12-15-203(a) (2015)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Ala. Code § 12-15-102(3) (2015)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ala. Code §
        15-27-1(a) (2015),Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - The case was dismissed with prejudice. [Ala. Code § 15-27-1(a)
        (2015),Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant is found not guilty. [Ala. Code § 15-27-1(a) (2015),Ala.
        Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]
      - One year has elapsed since the successful completion of a diversion
        program. [Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a)(3)(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Ala. Code § 15-27-4(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner does not owe any restitution, fines, court costs, late
        fees, or other fees. [Ala. Code § 15-27-12 (2015)]
      - The offense for which the person was arrested was not a violent offense.
        [Ala. Code § 15-27-2(a) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - Information not located in statutes.

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - The offense for which the pardon is being sought is not treason, nor is
        it a case of impeachment. [Ala. Const. amend. 38 (amending art. V § 124)
        (2015),Ala. Code § 15-22-36(a) (2015)]
      - In cases where the death sentence has been imposed, that sentence must
        first be commuted. [Ala. Code § 15-22-36(a) (2015)]
      - “No pardon shall be granted unless the prisoner has successfully
        completed at least three years of permanent parole or until the
        expiration of his or her sentence if his or her sentence was for less
        than three years.”. [Ala. Code § 15-22-36(c) (2015)]
      - “Notwithstanding the foregoing, a pardon based on innocence may be
        granted upon the unanimous affirmative vote of the board following
        receipt and filing of clear proof of his or her innocence of the crime
        for which he or she was convicted and the written approval of the judge
        who tried the case or district attorney or with the written approval of
        a circuit judge in the circuit where he or she was convicted if the
        judge who tried his or her case is dead or no longer serving.”. [Ala.
        Code § 15-22-36(c) (2015)]
      - The victim and any other interested party has been given written notice.
        [Ala. Code § 15-22-36(e)(1) (2015)]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [Ala. Code § 15-27-6(b) (2015)]

Misdemeanor Cases Received by the Prosecutor Resulting in Diversion

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Prosecutors. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.4(a) (2015)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Counties are authorized to implement any pretrial diversion program,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.1 (2015)]
      - Bad check programs. [Ala. Code § 12-17-224 (2015)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants charged with an eligible offense within a jurisdiction in
        which a pretrial diversion program exists. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2
        (2015),Ala. Code § 12-17-224(a)(2)(b) (2015)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Certain drug offenses. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(b) (2015)]
      - Property offenses. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(b) (2015)]
      - Offenses that do not involve serious physical injury, death, a victim
        under the age of 14, or a sex offense involving forcible compulsion or
        incapacity of a victim. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(b) (2015)]
      - All misdemeanors. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(b) (2015)]
      - Traffic or conservation offenses. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(b) (2015)]
      - Certain offenses related to issuing worthless checks or ceasing to make
        payments with the intention to defraud. [Ala. Code § 12-17-224(a)(2)(b)
        (2015)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Capital felonies. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Class A felonies. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Offenses involving serious physical injury to another person. [Ala. Code
        § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - An offense involving violence in which the victim was a child under 14
        years of age, a law enforcement officer, a school officer, a
        correctional officer, active duty military personnel of the United
        States military, or a person over the age of 65. [Ala. Code §
        12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Any offense involving death. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - A person deemed by the district attorney to be a threat to the safety or
        well-being of the community. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Bribery of a government or public official. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c)
        (2015)]
      - Any offense wherein the offender is a public official and the charge is
        related to their capacity as a public official. [Ala. Code §
        12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Abduction or kidnapping. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Any sex offense involving forcible compulsion or incapacity. [Ala. Code
        § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Certain offenses involving the production, possession, or dissemination
        of child pornography or other sex offenses against children involving
        the use of a computer. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Offenses related to judicial or other proceedings. [Ala. Code §
        12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Perjury. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Hate crimes. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]

    * Does state law allow pretrial diversion programs to charge a fee to
      defendants for admission and participation?

      - Yes. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.8(a) (2015)]

        § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]

        12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - Any offense involving death. [Ala. Code § 12-17-226.2(c) (2015)]
      - A person deemed by the district attorney to be a threat to the safety or

        (2015)]
